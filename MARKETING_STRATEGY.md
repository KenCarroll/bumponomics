# Bumponomics Marketing & Promotion Strategy

This document outlines the strategic approach for promoting *Bumponomics* across traditional and digital media channels. It serves as a living document to track marketing efforts, pitches, and media strategies.

## 1. The Core Philosophy
When pitching media (podcasts, radio, TV, print), the focus must shift from "author" to "subject matter expert."
*   **The Golden Rule:** Never pitch the book as the main story. Media outlets care about how your expertise can entertain, educate, or solve a problem for *their* audience.
*   **The Goal:** Provide immense value and insight during the interview, making the audience organically want to purchase *Bumponomics* to learn more.

## 2. The Media Kit ("Press Pass")
Before pitching, assemble a professional, downloadable media kit (hosted on the website).
*   **Professional Headshot**
*   **Short & Long Bios:** Establishing authority on Panarchy, systems theory, and the *Bumponomics* concepts.
*   **Book Details:** High-resolution cover image, blurb, release date, and purchasing links.
*   **Suggested Interview Questions:** Provide 5–10 engaging questions for producers (this reduces their workload and ensures the interview stays on-message).
*   **One-Sheet:** A highly visual, single-page PDF summarizing all the above.

## 3. Podcast Strategy
Podcasts are the most effective channel for non-fiction due to the long-form format and targeted audiences.
*   **Target Niches:** Economics, systems thinking, technology, futurism, and business strategy.
*   **Tiered Approach:** Start with smaller, niche podcasts to refine talking points before pitching top-tier shows.
*   **The Pitch Structure:**
    *   *Hook:* Reference a specific, recent episode of their show.
    *   *Value:* Offer to discuss 3 specific, counter-intuitive points related to *Bumponomics*.
    *   *Call to Action:* Attach the media kit and suggested questions.

## 4. Print & Digital Media
Securing write-ups in targeted publications.
*   **Newsjacking:** Monitor breaking news related to economic shifts or structural changes. Rapidly pitch journalists offering expert commentary through the lens of *Bumponomics*.
*   **Op-eds & Guest Articles:** Submit condensed versions of book chapters as opinion pieces to relevant magazines/blogs. Ensure your byline explicitly mentions the book and links back to the site.
*   **HARO (Connectively):** Monitor daily queries from journalists seeking expert quotes on business and economy.

## 5. Local TV and Radio
A stepping stone to national media and excellent for rapid, punchy segments.
*   **Local TV Morning Shows:** Pitch highly visual, digestible segments (e.g., "3 ways our local economy will shift according to new structural models").
*   **Drive-Time Radio:** Target morning/evening rush hour broadcasts. Producers prefer controversial, high-energy guests to engage commuters.
