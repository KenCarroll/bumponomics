## Starting with the End in Mind
Have been on a 10 year journey into "Problem-Solving Problem Solving".  The outputs from this are the development of a 'system'  (philosophy, language, thinking framework, methods, tools, and an application) aimed at navigating whatever problematic situations we encounter.  I've called this collective output "BUMPS" (Big Untamed Meaningful Problematic Situations). So for instance the framework is the "BUMPS Transformation System", and the application is called "www.bumps.app".  This approach builds upon many existing techniques and tools from across human learnings including most of the sciences.  One of the toolkits / approaches that it builds upon is called "Systems Thinking". Another is "Complexity Science".  As part of the journey have had some profound realisations / beliefs. One being that "All Systems are Problem Solving"...i.e. that all human systems, including organisations and their outputs (e.g. such as products/services, etc) are essentially a collection of responses to problematic situations.  That means to fully understand ANY human system (including societies, countries, businesses, families) and their outputs (economics, sciences, cities, products, services, etc) we need to first build an understanding of the problems (problematic situations) that caused humans to (1) pay attention (give it focus) (2) Give it time & energy (allocate their own time & resources to it) (3) Collaborate with others to formulate the 'problem' (often without any real structural thinking) (4) Create options for response (5) make choices (6) develop outputs based on those choices (7) Make progress (good or bad) (8) Iterate, fail, succeed, and learn etc.  To promote this 'seemingly uncommon' approach and to get critical feedback I want to submit this thinking in a paper to an upcoming "Systems Thinking Conference".  I want your help to draft the submission and supporting paper.  The paper will conclude that we can easily prove this 'thesis/hypothsis' by testing it against any existing human 'system' (organisation or their outputs) by identifying the problems upon which they are formed (imagine the problems forming a directed graph of some kind working back from the 'system' primary purpose to the underlying structures & behaviours that support its existence). Let's leverage Karl Popper's "All Life Is Problem Solving" and call our paper "All Systems are Problem Solving" ;-).
