# Bumponomics Conference Submission Draft

## 1. Abstract (< 250 words)

**Title: All Systems Are Problem Transforming: Introducing Bumponomics**

This paper introduces **Bumponomics**, a transdisciplinary meta-framework that redefines the purpose of all human systems as mechanisms for **Problem Transformation**. Challenging traditional Systems Thinking (ST) which focuses on function and interaction, this thesis posits that a system’s architecture can only be understood by mapping the **Problem System Network** it was created to solve.

We define these drivers as **BUMPS** (Big Untamed Meaningful Problematic Situations)—high-energy phenomena emerging from the collision zones of complex systems. Current organizational models suffer from a "Problem Blindspot," focusing on outputs (products, services) rather than the underlying **Problem Substrate**. This leads to fragility and operational misalignment.

Bumponomics operationalizes this theory through the **PTO Cycle** (Problems, Transformations, Outcomes). It provides a rigorous methodology for **"Problem-Solving Problem Solving,"** employing six transformation strategies: Absolve, Resolve, Dissolve, Solve, Evolve, and Exploit. By shifting focus from _intent_ (POSIWID) to _causal reality_ (OOSAWID—Outcomes Of a System Are What It Does), organizations can evolve into **Problem Transforming Organizations (PTOs)**.

This framework is currently being operationalized via **BUMPS.app**, an AI-enabled tool for mapping specific "BUMPScapes." We argue that viewing organizations through this problem-centric lens offers profound insights for strategic resilience in an increasingly complex world.
