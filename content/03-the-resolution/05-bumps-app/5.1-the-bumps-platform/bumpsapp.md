# BUMPS Application

We propose that the thesis, "All Systems are Problem Solving," can be empirically tested. This can be achieved by examining any existing human system (an organization, a product, a service, etc.) and identifying the sequence of problems upon which it was formed. We envision these problems as forming a directed graph, tracing back from the system's primary purpose to the underlying structures and behaviors that sustain its existence. Each node in this graph would represent a problematic situation, and the edges would illustrate the causal relationships between problems and the system's various components and actions. By mapping these problem-response pathways, we can demonstrate how systems are, at their core, elaborate solutions to identified problems.

## Introduction

BUMPS is a shared problem solving ‘system’. It helps people share & solve problems on-line and off-line, in situations involving multiple stakeholders.  It has been developed to help people think, decide and act.


It uses an easy to grasp vocabulary to help communicate complex human problems.  The aim is to create an enjoyable (playful) way to communicate stories, perspectives, behaviors, ingredients, relationships, patterns, events, and strategies. In this ‘system’ we use BUMPS as a metaphor, an acronym, and an abstract concept. 
- As a metaphor it represents challenges and obstacles people face as they progress.  
- As an acronym BUMPS are Big Unsolved Meaningful Problems worth Solving™ 
- As an abstract concept BUMPS have no direct representation in our physical world. But they are useful as a means to visualize & communicate.
