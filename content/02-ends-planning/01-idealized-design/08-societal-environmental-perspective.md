# 2.1.8 Societal/Environmental Perspective

Finally, the **Societal/Environmental Perspective** forces the design architecture to answer to the absolute boundaries of our reality. 

This lens demands that we address planetary-scale environmental constraints (oceans, land, air, and energy transition limits) and severe societal inequalities. In every crisis or BUMP, vulnerable groups—whether defined by geography, wealth, disability, demographic, or systemic historical bias—are fundamentally and disproportionately impacted. 

A future system cannot be "ideal-seeking" if its basic mechanics rely on externalizing costs onto the biosphere or exploiting marginalized populations. This perspective acts as the final moral and existential checkpoint: does the design ensure equity-focused problem transformation, and does it respect the terminal carrying capacity of Earth?
