# Cybernetics — Core Laws & Principles

Cybernetics is the study of **control**, **communication**, and **regulation** in systems (mechanical, biological, cognitive, social).  
There is no single canonical list of “laws,” but the following principles are widely accepted as the foundation of cybernetics.

---

## 1. Law of Requisite Variety (W. Ross Ashby)
> **“Only variety can absorb variety.”**

- A system can only regulate another system if it has **equal or greater behavioral variety**.
- If environmental complexity exceeds internal capacity, control fails.
- Core to resilience, governance, and adaptive design.

---

## 2. Good Regulator Theorem (Conant & Ashby)
> **“Every good regulator of a system must be a model of that system.”**

- Effective control requires an **internal model** of what is being regulated.
- Applies to brains, organizations, AI, and institutions.

---

## 3. Feedback & Circular Causality
> **Effects feed back as causes.**

- **Negative feedback** stabilizes (homeostasis).
- **Positive feedback** amplifies change (growth, collapse, tipping points).
- Linear causality is insufficient for understanding real systems.

---

## 4. Second-Order Cybernetics (Heinz von Foerster)
> **“The observer is part of the system.”**

- Observing or intervening changes the system.
- Essential for social systems, management, therapy, and ethics.
- Introduces self-reference and reflexivity.

---

## 5. Autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela)
> **Self-producing systems maintain their own organization.**

- Living and cognitive systems are **organizationally closed**.
- Systems do not just react; they actively maintain identity.

---

## 6. Law of Ultrastability (Ashby)
> **Systems survive by changing their internal structure.**

- When ordinary regulation fails, systems adapt at a deeper level.
- Learning, evolution, and reorganization enable long-term viability.

---

## 7. Variety Engineering (Stafford Beer)
- **Amplify internal variety** where control is needed.
- **Attenuate external variety** through filters, rules, and constraints.
- Central to the **Viable System Model (VSM)**.

---

## Key Takeaway
Cybernetics replaces linear control with **adaptive regulation**, **feedback**, and **self-modifying systems**.  
It applies wherever complexity must be managed—biology, organizations, AI, policy, and society itself.
