## Telos
The term "telos" refers to the ultimate purpose or goal of an action or entity. In the context of George Dyson's work, the "telos of meaningful behavior" can be understood through his statement: "Anything simple enough to be understandable will not be complicated enough to behave intelligently, while anything complicated enough to behave intelligently will not be simple enough to understand". 
This suggests that for a behavior to be truly intelligent or "meaningful" in a complex world, it must have an underlying purpose or goal ("telos") that cannot be easily simplified or fully understood through simple rules. Dyson's work often explores the complexity of systems, particularly in the realm of computers and technology, and the idea that true intelligence and purpose go beyond simple mechanisms or complete human comprehension. The purpose of meaningful behavior, therefore, lies in its inherent, complex, and potentially not fully knowable objective, rather than a simple, predefined function.

##  Enabling Constraints
Enabling constraints are rules or boundaries that, paradoxically, limit actions in a way that stimulates creativity, novelty, and innovation by forcing new solutions or aligning efforts, rather than stifling them. Think of them as "guardrails" that guide progress and prevent disastrous outcomes, allowing for emergent solutions in complex situations where rigid rules fail, such as Kanban's WIP limits or the historical creation of Fanta during resource scarcity.
