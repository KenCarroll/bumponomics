BUMPS Strategy (North Star)

## BUMPS Strategy

## Strategy Alignment 
Steer towards the average heading (velocity) of local flockmates.

## Strategy Cohesion
Steer towards the average position (center of mass) of local flockmates.

## Strategy Seperation
Steer to avoid crowding local flockmates.


## Extended Boid Rules 
These rules are extensions to the three core Boids rules (separation, alignment, cohesion) and allow the simulated agents (boids) to interact with their environment and specific targets. 

## Obstacle Avoidance: 
This rule enables boids to detect and steer around physical objects in their environment to prevent collisions.
- Mechanism: Boids use a form of "vision" (e.g., raycasting or a defined proximity sensor range) to detect obstacles in their path or nearby.
- Action: If an obstacle is detected within a certain distance or angle, the boid generates a force or steering command that points away from the obstacle, often prioritizing this rule over all others to ensure safety.

## Goal Seeking: 
This rule directs the flock to move toward a specific, predefined destination or target point in the environment.
- Mechanism: A force or acceleration vector is calculated that steers the boid toward the target's pos  ition.
- Action: This behavior typically has a lower priority than obstacle and predator avoidance, but works with the core flocking rules to ensure the group as a whole moves in the desired general direction over time while maintaining flock integrity and avoiding collisions.

## Predator Avoidance: 
This rule causes boids (prey) to detect and flee from agents designated as predators.
- Mechanism: Boids have a "fleeing range" within which they can detect predators. When a predator is detected, the boid calculates a force that is the inverse of "goal seeking" (i.e., it steers directly away from the predator).
- Action: This force is often strong and overrides or takes high priority over other behaviors, such as cohesion or alignment, causing the flock to disperse temporarily to escape the threat. This can also be contagious, causing nearby boids to flee even if they haven't directly sensed the predator.
