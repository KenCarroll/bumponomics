# Cadences (Rhythms)

The PTO Process does not have a fixed cadence. It has a natural cadence to it. It depends upon the nature of the problems being addressed in relation to the scope and scale of its environment.

For example, a HUMP (macro level) problem will take longer to address than a BUMP (meso level) problem. A BUMP will take longer to address than a LUMP (micro level) problem.

In the case of a HUMP, the cadence will be slow and steady. In the case of a BUMP, the cadence will be faster and more dynamic. In the case of a LUMP, the cadence will be fast and chaotic.

Examples:

- HUMP: Climate Change (Decades)
- BUMP: AI Safety (Years)
- LUMP: Vaccine Safety (Months)
