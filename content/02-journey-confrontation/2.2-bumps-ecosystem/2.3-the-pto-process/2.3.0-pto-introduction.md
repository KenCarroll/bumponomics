#PTO Process 

The PTO Framework is a sophisticated mental model that blends Systems Thinking, Design Thinking, and Cybernetics. It describes a self-reinforcing loop of action and outcomes that results in progress. Progress can be be "+" or "-" depending upon the nature of the problem formulation, the intent, the transformations, and the focus on outcomes. 

PTO framework provides a structure (the diamonds) and a vocabulary (the strategies) for people to organize their own thoughts. It's a Cognitive Architecture: a way to view the world rather than just a way to do work, it is an architecture for progress.

PTO model follows a Triple Diamond structure. While the "Double Diamond" is standard in design (Discover/Define, Develop/Deliver), PTO adds a third phase for the systemic aftermath.


The **PTO Framework** is a generative, triple-diamond methodology used to navigate complex systems. It acknowledges that progress is not a linear path to an "end," but a continuous loop of transformation where outcomes inevitably seed the next set of problematic situations.

---

## I. The Process: The Triple Diamond

The framework moves through three distinct phases, each utilizing a cycle of **Divergence** (expanding the view) and **Convergence** (focusing the intent).

### 1. Problems (P)
* **Diverge:** Discover the "Problematic Situations." Explore the mess, symptoms, and various stakeholder perspectives without judgment.
* **Converge:** Define the specific "Points of Action." Narrow down the complexity into defined, actionable problems where you have the  agency and capability to intervene.

### 2. Transformations (T)
* **Diverge:** Define & evaluate the problem through the **6 -volves** (The Strategies). Consider the implications of each approach.
* **Converge:** Select and deliver the specific Transformation strategy that aligns with your resources and intent.

### 3. Outcomes (O)
* **Diverge:** Observe the "Emergent Properties." Identify all intended and unintended consequences of the transformation.
* **Converge:** Focus on the "Meaningful Outcomes." These pros and cons define your new reality and form the basis for the next "Problematic Situation."

## II. The 6 Transformations (The Strategy Palette)

During the **T** phase, use these six distinct lenses to determine how to handle the problem.

| Strategy | Action | Mindset | The Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Absolve** | **Ignore** | *Laissez-faire* | The problem is left to run its course or self-correct. |
| **Resolve** | **Patch** | Pragmatic | A "good enough" solution that suppresses symptoms. |
| **Solve** | **Optimize** | Clinical | A data-driven "ideal" fix to return to a known state. |
| **Dissolve** | **Redesign** | Architectural | The system is changed so the problem cannot exist. |
| **Evolve** | **Transcend** | Evolutionary | The problem acts as a catalyst for a higher state of being. |
| **Revolve** | **Fuel** | **Antagonist** | The problem is fed energy to keep it in a stable, profitable orbit. |

---

## III. Diagnostic: The "Revolve" Red Flags

The **Revolve** strategy is used by antagonists to ensure the loop remains a circle rather than a spiral. If you suspect a problem is being intentionally "Revolved," look for these indicators:

* **The Circular Feedback:** Do the outcomes of the "fix" lead directly back to the original problem with no systemic change?
* **The Resource Leak:** Is there a specific actor who gains funding, power, or relevance only as long as this problem exists?
* **The "Crisis Fuel":** Is just enough energy being provided to keep the problem from exploding, but not enough to actually solve it?

> **The PTO Motto:** *We don't solve problems to end them; we transform them to evolve the system.*

---

## IV. Quick-Start Guide
1.  **Look at the mess (P).** Pick the part you can touch.
2.  **Pick your "V" (T).** Are you Solving, Dissolving, or is an antagonist Revolving?
3.  **Watch what happens (O).** What is the new "Problematic Situation" created by your action?
4.  **Repeat.**
