# WHO'S R-ARSED


## R-ARSED – Comparative Table

| Play           | Energy Requirement                       | Time Horizon            | Incentives / Motivation                    | Ethics / System Impact                                                 |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Absolving [A]  | Low – withdraw effort                    | Immediate to indefinite | Cost–benefit dictates inaction             | Ethical if proportional; problem persists but harmless                 |
| Resolving [R]  | Medium – repeated interventions          | Short to medium         | Legitimacy, precedent, efficiency          | Maintains system; problem temporarily controlled, may recur            |
| Solving [S]    | High – analytical and maintenance effort | Medium to long          | Reduce recurrence, restore system function | Partial improvement; problem masked but controlled, energy-intensive   |
| Evolving [E]   | Medium – adaptation over time            | Long-term               | Growth, learning, resilience               | System transforms; problem absorbed sustainably, ethically positive    |
| Dissolving [D] | High upfront, then low                   | One-time, permanent     | Remove root conditions                     | Problem eliminated; systemic redesign, high ethical impact             |
| Revolving [R]  | High ongoing – active maintenance        | Indefinite              | Power, attention, revenue, control         | Exploitative; problem persists for actor advantage, ethically negative |
