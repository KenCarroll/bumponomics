**POSIWID vs. OOSIWID: A Shift in Systems Thinking**

Stafford Beer’s famous "The Purpose Of a System Is What It Does" (POSIWID) is a cornerstone of cybernetics and systems thinking. It compels us to look beyond stated intent and identify a system’s true purpose by observing its output.

But does POSIWID always capture the full picture? I'd argue no.

The current actions of a system may not reflect its intended purpose if the system is:

* In development.  
* In an error state.  
* Distorted (e.g., "hacked" or subject to external pressures).

In these scenarios, what the system *does* is a function of its *current state*, which can be misaligned with its original intent.

Furthermore **systems produce intended and untended outcomes**. Emergent properties are unpredictable in their operating environments. The primary purpose of an automobile is personal transportation. But what emerges, the outcomes (plus/minus) are numerous (economic growth,  environmental harm, etc). **There is, at best, only a partial  connection between its purpose and what it actually does**.

💡 Introducing **OOSIWID: "Outcomes Of a System Is What It Does."**

OOSIWID acknowledges that the system’s actions are simply its current, real-world results. By shifting the focus from "Purpose" to "Outcomes," we gain a more accurate, less judgmental measure of performance.

The distinction is subtle but critical:**POSIWID (Purpose Of a System Is What It Does)**

* **Focus:** Purpose  
* **Implication:** The system's output reveals its true, underlying intent.  
* **Diagnostic Use:** What is this system *really* for?

**OOSIWID (Outcomes Of a System Is What It Does)**

* **Focus:** Outcomes  
* **Implication:** The system's output is its current, observable result—regardless of intent.  
* **Diagnostic Use:** How large is the gap between intended purpose and actual result?

The Value of OOSIWID: It constantly measures the gap between the system's intended purpose and its actual, messy results. It becomes a diagnostic tool that highlights true performance, rather than just labeling the output as the inherent purpose.
