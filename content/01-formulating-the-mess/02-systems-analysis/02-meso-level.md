# 1.2.2 The Meso Level: Institutions & Industries

When we scale up from the firm to entire sectors of the economy, the Micro incentives consolidate into institutional regimes. This is the Meso level.

## Institutional Moats and Artificial Missions

At the Meso level, industries form defensive moats designed explicitly to reject environmental turbulence. Cybernetically, instead of adapting to an influx of external variety, institutions use their power to forcibly homogenize the environment. Through regulatory capture, cartel-like behaviors, and rigid standardization, they attempt to lock the environment into a manageable, static state.

When genuine crises arise, these institutional monoliths inevitably refuse to address the underlying structural problems. Doing so would require them to cannibalize their own operations. Instead, they launch highly publicized **Missions**—massive, top-down initiatives that simulate problem-solving while carefully sidestepping the root causes that threaten their dominance.

The Meso system is thereby characterized by an immense, deeply entrenched resistance. It is an architecture of stability that treats dynamic problem-solving as an existential threat.
