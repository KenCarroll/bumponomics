Title: BUMPONOMICS : Using Idealized Design to Re-Imagine a New Economics System
Type: Poster Submission
Author: Ken Carroll
Affiliation: BUMPCONDUCTOR BV / SCiO Member
Work Count = 216

Abstract:
The current economic reality, defined by rapid and complex disruptions, renders "business as usual" a path to failure. Traditional economic thinking treats problems as externalities; however, we posit that problems are, in fact, key drivers of our wellbeing. Using Russel Ackoff’s Idealized Design and Interactive planning techniques, this poster introduces Bumponomics, an economic paradigm that focuses on Problem Transformation as the key driver of progress. 

Current economics, with measures such as GDP (Gross Domestic Product), measures growth through the volume of transactions but neglects their value to human viability. We posit that the fundamental unit of value is the Transformed Problem, not currency. Bumponomics replaces the pursuit of profit with three evolutionary metrics:
GMP (Gross Meaningful Problems): Measuring ambition—are we allocating resources to existential opportunities or misplaced trivialities?
GPO (Gross Positive Outcomes): Measuring efficacy—did our transformations actually progress the problem without creating negative displacement?
GIVE (Gross Increased Variety of Environment): Measuring legacy—did we expand the "library of parts" and future evolutionary potential?
Shifting focus from product (GDP) to progress (GMP/GPO/GIVE) initiates a transition from a materials-based Economy to a progress Economy.  Realisation of Bumponomics is driven by BUMPS PTO process (Problems ➔ Transformations ➔ Outcomes), a form of interactive planning for tackling systems-of-problems (BUMPS). These BUMPONOMICS fundamentals, methods, and tools provide the compass and "physics" for realizing this new economy.
